The Whisper in the Garden

by Jane Doe

It was a quiet morning when Lily stepped into the garden, dew shimmering on the petals around her. She felt a gentle breeze brush her cheek, as if the flowers whispered her name.

Curious, she leaned closer to a rose and heard:

"Welcome, dear friend. Stay awhile."

Lily smiled and settled on a stone bench, the air fragrant with blooming jasmine. All around, the flowers swayed in rhythm, humming an unspoken melody.

As sunlight scattered through the leaves, she closed her eyes. In that moment, the garden and Lily were one—connected by a soft secret shared only in silence.

The whisper faded, but Lily carried its memory with her—into each new day.
